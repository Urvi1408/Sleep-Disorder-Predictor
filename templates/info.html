🛏️ Insomnia

📖 What is Insomnia?

Insomnia is a common sleep disorder that makes it difficult to fall asleep, stay asleep, or get restful sleep even when enough time is available for sleep. It may be short-term (acute) or long-term (chronic).

⚠️ Common Symptoms

  • Difficulty falling asleep.
  • Waking up frequently during the night.
  • Waking up too early.
  • Feeling tired after waking up.
  • Daytime sleepiness and fatigue.
  • Difficulty concentrating.
  • Mood changes or irritability.

🧬 Possible Causes

  • Stress and anxiety.
  • Depression.
  • Irregular sleep schedule.
  • Excessive caffeine or alcohol.
  • Poor sleeping environment.
  • Certain medications.
  • Medical conditions such as chronic pain.

💡 Prevention & Healthy Habits

  • Maintain a regular sleep schedule.
  • Avoid screens before bedtime.
  • Reduce caffeine intake in the evening.
  • Exercise regularly.
  • Keep your bedroom cool, dark, and quiet.
  • Practice relaxation techniques before sleeping.

🩺 When to Seek Medical Help

If insomnia lasts for several weeks, affects your daily activities, or causes severe daytime fatigue, consult a qualified healthcare professional.

😴 Sleep Apnea

📖 What is Sleep Apnea?

Sleep apnea is a sleep disorder in which breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep. These interruptions reduce oxygen levels and prevent restful sleep, often causing daytime tiredness.

⚠️ Common Symptoms

  • Loud snoring.
  • Pauses in breathing during sleep.
  • Gasping or choking while sleeping.
  • Morning headaches.
  • Dry mouth after waking.
  • Excessive daytime sleepiness.
  • Poor concentration.

🧬 Possible Causes

  • Excess body weight.
  • Narrow airway.
  • Enlarged tonsils.
  • Smoking.
  • Alcohol consumption.
  • Family history.
  • Increasing age.

💡 Prevention & Healthy Habits

  • Maintain a healthy body weight.
  • Sleep on your side instead of your back.
  • Avoid smoking and alcohol before bedtime.
  • Exercise regularly.
  • Follow a consistent sleep schedule.
  • Consult a healthcare professional if symptoms continue.

🩺 When to Seek Medical Help

If you experience loud snoring, repeated breathing pauses during sleep, or excessive daytime sleepiness, consult a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.

🌙 Healthy Sleep

📖 What Does It Mean?

A prediction of No Sleep Disorder indicates that your sleep patterns appear healthy based on the information provided. Maintaining healthy sleep habits helps support your physical, mental, and emotional well-being.

✅ Healthy Sleep Habits

  • Sleep 7–9 hours every night.
  • Go to bed and wake up at the same time daily.
  • Exercise regularly.
  • Eat a balanced diet.
  • Manage stress effectively.
  • Limit screen time before bedtime.
  • Keep your bedroom cool, dark, and quiet.

🌟 Why Good Sleep Matters

Quality sleep helps improve memory, concentration, mood, immune function, and overall health. It also supports your body's recovery and reduces the risk of several chronic diseases.

💡 Healthy Sleep Tips

🌙 Maintain a Sleep Schedule

Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends, to help regulate your body's internal clock.

📵 Reduce Screen Time

Avoid using phones, laptops, or other electronic devices at least 30 minutes before bedtime, as blue light can interfere with sleep.

🛏️ Create a Comfortable Sleep Environment

Keep your bedroom cool, dark, and quiet to promote deeper and more restful sleep throughout the night.
